Cybersecurity Risk Rules Checklist

1 Access Control Check
1.1 Ensure strong password policies (e.g., length, complexity, expiration).
1.2 Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for critical systems.
1.3 Regularly review and update user access rights based on roles and responsibilities.
1.4 Monitor and restrict physical access to sensitive areas and devices.
1.5 Enable port security to allow only one device and shutdown others.
1.6 Restrict access on routers for trainees.
1.7 Implement fingerprint access control.
1.8 Maintain social distancing protocols.
1.9 Avoid discussing confidential or sensitive information in the presence of visitors unless authorized.
2 Data Protection Check
2.1 Encrypt sensitive data both in transit and at rest.
2.2 Establish data classification policies and ensure appropriate handling based on sensitivity.
2.3 Implement data loss prevention (DLP) mechanisms to prevent unauthorized data leakage.
2.4 Regularly backup critical data and ensure backups are stored securely.
2.5 Use hashing to verify file integrity.
2.6 Establish data retention and disposal policies to securely manage data lifecycle.
2.7 Enforce the principle of least privilege to limit access to only the necessary data required to perform job functions.
2.8 Regularly review and audit user access permissions to ensure compliance with security policies.
2.9 Implement key management practices to securely generate, store, and rotate encryption keys.
2.10 Anonymize personal data where feasible to reduce the risk of unauthorized disclosure.
2.11 Implement dynamic data masking to restrict access to sensitive data based on user roles and permissions.
3 Network Security Check
3.1 Utilize Firewalls to monitor and control network traffic.
3.2 Segment networks to limit the impact of a breach and prevent lateral movement by attackers.
3.3 Regularly scan and patch systems for vulnerabilities.
3.4 Implement network monitoring and logging to detect suspicious activities.
3.5 Use AAA for secure login to emails and accounts.
3.6 Deploy IPS/IDS for intrusion detection and prevention.
3.7 Implement a honey bot server to deceive attackers and gather intelligence.
3.8 Employ antivirus/antimalware software on servers.
3.9 Configure WSA & WAF for enhanced security of web servers.
3.10 Employ ESA to protect email servers.
3.11 Use proxies for private IP access.
3.12 Provide VPN access for remote employees.
3.13 Conduct network scans using Nessus.
3.14 Manage network effectively with Cisco DNA.
3.15 Utilize Active Directory for centralized device control.
3.16 Encrypt router passwords.
3.17 Regularly scan and patch network devices and systems for vulnerabilities.
4 Endpoint Security Check
4.1 Deploy antivirus/antimalware solutions and keep them up to date.
4.2 Enforce endpoint encryption for laptops, mobile devices, and other endpoints.
4.3 Implement endpoint detection and response solutions for real-time threat detection and response.
4.4 Establish and enforce policies for remote access and mobile device management .
4.5 Enable host-based firewall protection.
4.6 Encourage the use of password managers.
4.7 Ensure operating systems are regularly updated.
4.8 Monitor and log endpoint activity for signs of compromise.
4.9 Set exec-timeout for device security.
4.10 Configure automatic screen lock on work computers.
5 Personnel Security Check
5.1 Issue and enforce ID badges for all staff.
5.2 Include current photos on ID badges.
5.3 Clearly indicate access levels on badges.
5.4 Implement background check policies.
5.5 Establish termination protocols for access removal.
6 Incident Response Check
6.1 Develop and regularly test an incident response plan.
6.2 Assign roles and responsibilities for incident response, including communication protocols.
6.3 Establish procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents.
6.4 Maintain relationships with law enforcement, incident response teams, and relevant vendors for support during incidents.
6.5 Establish procedures for reporting phishing attempts.
6.6 Identify potential security risks and threats to the organization's assets, including data, systems, and physical infrastructure.
6.7 Prioritize risks based on their severity and potential consequences.
6.8 Develop and implement strategies to mitigate identified risks.
6.9 Develop and maintain business continuity and disaster recovery plans to ensure the organization can recover from security incidents or disasters quickly.
6.10 Test and update these plans regularly to ensure effectiveness.
7 Security Awareness and Training Check
7.1 Conduct regular security awareness training for all employees.
7.2 Ensure employees understand common threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering) and how to respond.
7.3 Test employees' awareness through simulated phishing campaigns and other exercises.
7.4 Encourage a culture of security awareness and proactive reporting of security concerns.
7.5 Train employees to recognize and prevent human errors.
7.6 Educate employees on the importance of security policies.
7.7 Establish workload management strategies.
7.8 Educate employees on social engineering tactics.
7.9 Implement measures to prevent abuse of privileges.
7.10 Educate clients on the importance of safeguarding sensitive information and reporting any security concerns or incidents.
8 Third-Party Risk Management Check
8.1 Assess and regularly review the security practices of third-party vendors and partners.
8.2 Establish contractual agreements that define security responsibilities and liabilities.
8.3 Monitor third-party access and activities within the organization's network.
8.4 Have a process for promptly addressing security incidents involving third parties.
8.5 Ensure third parties adhere to security standards and protocols.
9 Regulatory Compliance Check
9.1 Stay updated on relevant regulations and compliance requirements (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS).
9.2 Conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.
9.3 Document and maintain records of compliance efforts and security controls.
9.4 Establish procedures for reporting breaches to regulatory authorities and affected individuals.
9.5 Ensure SSL certificates are up to date.
9.6 Monitor for new vulnerabilities and emerging threats relevant to your organization.
10 Continuous Monitoring and Improvement Check
10.1 Implement continuous monitoring tools and processes to detect and respond to evolving threats.
10.2 Regularly review and update security policies and procedures based on changes in the threat landscape and business needs.
10.3 Conduct post-incident reviews and lessons learned sessions to improve future incident response efforts.
10.4 Stay informed about emerging cybersecurity trends, technologies, and best practices.
10.5 Monitor premises with CCTV.
10.6 Enable NTP, VRF, and SNMP protocols.
10.7 Monitor and log changes made to controllers over the network.
10.6 Log changes made to controllers via physical connections.
11 Backup and Disaster Recovery Check
11.1 Regularly backup critical systems and data.
11.2 Store backups securely in off-site locations.
11.3 Test backup and recovery procedures regularly to ensure they are effective.
11.4 Have a documented disaster recovery plan outlining procedures for restoring operations in the event of a major disruption or disaster.
12 Visitor Access Check
12.1 Implement access control for visitor .
12.2 Require ID presentation for all visitors.
12.3 Issue visitor passes with appropriate permissions.
12.4 Designate specific areas for visitors.
12.5 Use X-ray machines or metal detectors for thorough inspections, if necessary.
12.6 Visitors should sign non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) or confidentiality agreements before accessing sensitive areas or information.
13 Electronic Security Check
13.1 Ensure proper lighting for surveillance.
13.2 Install and maintain surveillance cameras.
13.3 Regularly check camera functionality.
13.4 There is an electricity reserve in case of a power outage.
