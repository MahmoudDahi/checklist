Cybersecurity Risk Rules Checklist

1 Access Control Check
1.1 Ensure strong password policies (e.g., length, complexity, expiration).
1.2 Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for critical systems.
1.3 Regularly review and update user access rights based on roles and responsibilities.
1.4 Monitor and restrict physical access to sensitive areas and devices.
2 Data Protection Check
2.1 Encrypt sensitive data both in transit and at rest.
2.2 Establish data classification policies and ensure appropriate handling based on sensitivity.
2.3 Implement data loss prevention (DLP) mechanisms to prevent unauthorized data leakage.
2.4 Regularly backup critical data and ensure backups are stored securely.
3 Network Security Check
3.1 Employ firewalls and intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS) to monitor and control network traffic.
3.2 Segment networks to limit the impact of a breach and prevent lateral movement by attackers.
3.3 Regularly scan and patch systems for vulnerabilities.
3.4 Implement network monitoring and logging to detect suspicious activities.
4 Endpoint Security Check
4.1 Deploy antivirus/antimalware solutions and keep them up to date.
4.2 Enforce endpoint encryption for laptops, mobile devices, and other endpoints.
4.3 Implement endpoint detection and response solutions for real-time threat detection and response.
4.4 Establish and enforce policies for remote access and mobile device management .
5 Incident Response Check
5.1 Develop and regularly test an incident response plan.
5.2 Assign roles and responsibilities for incident response, including communication protocols.
5.3 Establish procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents.
5.4 Maintain relationships with law enforcement, incident response teams, and relevant vendors for support during incidents.
6 Security Awareness and Training Check
6.1 Conduct regular security awareness training for all employees.
6.2 Ensure employees understand common threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering) and how to respond.
6.3 Test employees' awareness through simulated phishing campaigns and other exercises.
6.4 Encourage a culture of security awareness and proactive reporting of security concerns.
7 Third-Party Risk Management Check
7.1 Assess and regularly review the security practices of third-party vendors and partners.
7.2 Establish contractual agreements that define security responsibilities and liabilities.
7.3 Monitor third-party access and activities within the organization's network.
7.4 Have a process for promptly addressing security incidents involving third parties.
8 Regulatory Compliance Check
8.1 Stay updated on relevant regulations and compliance requirements (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS).
8.2 Conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.
8.3 Document and maintain records of compliance efforts and security controls.
8.4 Establish procedures for reporting breaches to regulatory authorities and affected individuals.
9 Continuous Monitoring and Improvement Check
9.1 Implement continuous monitoring tools and processes to detect and respond to evolving threats.
9.2 Regularly review and update security policies and procedures based on changes in the threat landscape and business needs.
9.3 Conduct post-incident reviews and lessons learned sessions to improve future incident response efforts.
9.4 Stay informed about emerging cybersecurity trends, technologies, and best practices.
10 Backup and Disaster Recovery Check
10.1 Regularly backup critical systems and data.
10.2 Store backups securely in off-site locations.
10.3 Test backup and recovery procedures regularly to ensure they are effective.
10.4 Have a documented disaster recovery plan outlining procedures for restoring operations in the event of a major disruption or disaster.
